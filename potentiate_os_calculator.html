Equity and stock compensation — what this means in practice

Equity is a standard part of VP-level offer packages and worth understanding before you negotiate. According to Carta, the market standard is a 4-year vesting schedule with a 1-year cliff. No equity is accessible in year one. After 12 months the first portion vests. The rest follows gradually over 3 years.

Two types appear most often at the VP level. RSUs are grants of actual company stock. At public companies they have real cash value when they vest. At private companies equity typically comes as stock options — significant on paper but dependent on a future liquidity event like an acquisition or IPO to realize actual value.

Sequoia's 2025 compensation data notes that without a clear liquidity path, equity awards carry more risk at private companies. The figures shown here are estimated annual grant values — a negotiation component, not a guaranteed take-home number. Understanding the range helps you know what to ask for when an offer is in front of you.
